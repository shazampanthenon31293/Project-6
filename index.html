TITLE I—Change to Sub-Committees

SECTION 101. Judciary Committee.

The Judicary Committee role is to review pending legislation, consider executive nominations (only the senate). The Judicary Committee also overseeing the administration of justice within the federal courts, administrative agencies and Federal law enforcement entities

(a) House Committee.— Judiciary now has the following sub-committees

(1) Antitrust, Commercial and Administrative Law

(2) The Constitution, Civil Rights and Civil Liberties

(3) Crime, Terrorism and Homeland Security

(4) Immigration and Citizenship

(5) Courts, Intellectual Property and the Internet

(6) Oversight

(b) Senate.— Judiciary now has the following sub-committees

(1) Antitrust, Competition Policy and Consumer Rights

(2) The Constitution

(3) Crime and Terrorism

(4) Border Security and Immigration

(5) Federal Courts

(6) Intellectual Property

(7) Oversight



(c) All Committees- have to follow the procedures outlined below

(1) Chairman and Ranking Member

(A) The Chairman has to be

(i) Member of the Full Committee;

(ii) Member of the Majority Caucus or Confrence;

(iii) Not a chairman of any other Judicary sub-committee;

(I) Unless given a signed waiver. The waiver has to be signed by both the leader of your party and the whip of your party

(II) The Waiver has to be entered into the congressional record

(B) The Ranking Member has to be

(i) Member of the Full Committee;

(ii) Member of the Minority Caucus or Confrence;

(iii) Not a ranking member of any other Judicary sub-committee;

(I) Unless given a signed waiver. The waiver has to be signed by both the leader of your party and the whip of your party

(II) The Waiver has to be entered into the congressional record

(C) Membership

(i) House Sub-Committee

(I) Majority Party: 9 members that is including chairman (All have to be members of the full committee)

(II) Minority Party: 8 members that is including ranking members (All have to be members of the full committee)

(ii) Senate Sub-Committee

(I) Majority Party: 5 members that is including chairman (All have to be members of the full committee)

(II) Minority Party: 4 members that is including ranking members (All have to be members of the full committee)

How members shall be appointed by both the Majority and Minority leaders in both houses:

(a) Appointed by their party leadership

(b) Approved by the caucus (or confrence) of the party leader

(c) Fully voted on by only the caucus (or confrence) of the party leader (formality)

Powers of Committees and Sub-Committees

(a) Issue Subpeonas for

(1) subpoena ad testificandum or a subpeona for testimony

(2) subpoena duces tecum or a subpeona for physical evidence

(b) Oversee legislation sent to the committee

(c) Send Legislation to the whole floor for a vote


SECTION 102. Foreign Affairs Committee.

The Foreign Affairs (Or Foreign Relations in the Senate) Committee is generally responsible for overseeing (but not administering) and funding foreign aid programs as well as funding arms sales and training for national allies they also have jurisdiction over bills and investigations related to the foreign affairs of the United States

(a) House Committee.— Foreign Affairs now has the following sub-committees

(1) Middle East

(2) Sub-Saharan Africa

(3) Western Europe

(5) East and Southeast Asia

(7) South Asia

(8) North Asia

(9) Eastern Europe

(10) Americas (Excepting the US)

(11) Energy, International Terrorism and the Environment

(12) Oversight and Investigations

(b) Senate.— Foreign Relations now has the following sub-committees

(1) Afro-Eurasia

(2) Western Hemisphere, Transnational Crime, Civilian Security, Democracy, Human Rights and Global Women's Issues

(3) International Cybersecurity Policy

(4) Africa, East Asia, The Pacific, Global Health Policy and International Economic, Energy and Environmental Policy

SECTION 103. Appropriations.

The committee is responsible for passing appropriation bills.The bills passed by the Appropriations Committee regulate expenditures of money by the government of the United States. As such, it is one of the most powerful of the committees, and its members are seen as influential. They make the key decisions about the work of their committees—when their committees meet, which bills they will consider, and for how long.
