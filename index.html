To Reorganize the Senate and the House Committees. Also to provide for increased efficiency in the legislative branch of the Government, also for creating a couple of joint committees and establishing their purpouses.

IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES

________,____

Ms. Lofgren(for herself and Mr. Kilmer, Mr. Johnson of Wisconsin, Mr. Peters of Michigan, Mr. Davis of Illinois, Mr. Graves of Georgia, Ms. Maloney and Mr. Jordan) introduced the following bill; which was read twice and referred to the Select Committee on Modernization of Congress, Committee on Oversight and Reform, Committee on House Administration, Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs; for further consideration

A BILL

A bill to abolish all of the existing congressional committees and subcommittes and then create all new committees and sub-committees and creates new joint-committees and who sits on theme

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled,

SECTION 1. Short title; table of contents.

(a) Short title.—This Act may be cited as the "Legislative reorginization act of 2021".

(b) Committee.—All congressional committees are hereby abolished

SECTION 1001. Senate Judicary.

(a) Chairman.— The Chairman of the Senate Judiciary Committee has to be

(1) A Member of the Majority Caucus or Confrence

(2) A Served in at-least one congress before you are appointed chairman (by that i mean is one-third of a full term for senator)

(3) He or She can not be a chairman of any judicary sub-committee with a waiver from party leadership

(A) Leadership mean the Senate Majority Leader and the Senate Majority Whip

(B) The Waiver has to be approved by the Majority Caucus not the whole Senate

(C) If you get the Waiver it has to be posted by Government Publishing Office (GPO)

(4) He or She must be a senator from one of the states


(b) Ranking Member.— The Ranking Member of the Senate Judiciary Committee has to be

(1) A Member of the Minority Caucus or Confrence

(2) A Served in at-least one congress before you are appointed ranking member (by that i mean is one-third of a full term for senator)

(3) He or Sh can not be a ranking member of any judicary sub-committee with a waiver from party leadership

(A) Leadership mean the Senate Minority Leader and the Senate Minority Whip

(B) The Waiver has to be approved by the Minority Caucus not the whole Senate

(C) If you get the Waiver it has to be posted by Government Publishing Office (GPO)

(4) He or She must be a senator from one of the states


(c) Jursidiction.— The jursdiction of the Senate Judiciary Committee shall be

(1) Executive  Nomination- They consider executive nominations

(A) Positions like

(i) Attorney General;

(ii) Deputy Attorney General;

(iii) Associate Attorney General;

(iv) Assistant Attorney General;

(v) One of the 93 United States Attorneys;

(vi) One of the 94 United States Marshals;

(vii) Supreme Court Justices;

(viii) Federal Judges;

(2) Oversight- The committee has oversight over the following departements

(A) Justice Departement inlcuding the FBI (Federal Bureau of Investigation), DEA (Drug Enforcement Administration). It also has oversight of the Department of Homeland Security (DHS).

(3) Other- They also have jursdiction over

(A) It also has jursidiction considering proposed constitutional amendments and legislation related to federal criminal law, human rights law, immigration, intellectual property, antitrust law, and internet privacy.



(d) Membership.— This committee shall have the following compoisition

(1) Majority- The Majority shall have 12 members

(1) Minority- The Minority shall have 10 members
